Sir Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman

Nobel Laureate in Physics (1930) - Discoverer of the Raman Effect

About C. V. Raman:

Sir C. V. Raman was an Indian physicist renowned for his groundbreaking work in the field of light scattering. He discovered the Raman Effect, which describes the change in the wavelength of light when it is deflected by molecules. This discovery earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1930, making him the first Asian to receive a Nobel in any branch of science.
