The croissant's history begins in Austria with the kipferl, a crescent-shaped pastry dating back to at least the 13th century. While a popular legend suggests the kipferl was created to celebrate Vienna's victory over the Ottoman Empire, it's more likely that the croissant evolved from the kipferl. August Zang, an Austrian, introduced the kipferl to Paris in 1838, establishing a Viennese bakery.
